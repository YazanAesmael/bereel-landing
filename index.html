01 — The case against

The feed is no longer from the people.

The internet was promised as a library. It has become a slot machine. Every major platform optimizes for the same thing: seconds spent looking. The metric rewards outrage, repetition, and now, endless synthetic generation.

We are drowning in AI slop. It is cheap to make, impossible to verify, and designed to hold your attention long enough to serve an ad. It buries the work of people who actually made something.

The platform you loved was sold while you slept, to people who never asked what you wanted, for reasons you will never be told. Your politics, your ethnicity, your orientation — filed, stored, and sold back to whoever is paying this week.

BeReel is a refusal. A correction.

02 — Three rules

Non-negotiable.

01.

No uploads.

There is no upload button. Every video is captured live through the BeReel camera. If it wasn't recorded here, it can't be posted here. This is not a content policy. It is the architecture of the app.

02.

No AI.

Because there are no uploads, there is no path for generated video to reach the feed. No AI-generated clips. No AI-cloned voices. No AI-written scripts read by a synthetic face. Only what the camera saw.

03.

No games.

No shadowbans. No pay-to-play. No invisible throttling. The feed ranks by recency and engagement, and the formula is public. We do not sell your data. We do not sell your attention. We do not sell you.

03 — If you make things

For creators.

Your work is indistinguishable from a prompt. That ends here.

You spent two days on an edit. A content farm spent four minutes on theirs, and the algorithm can't tell the difference. Neither, anymore, can most of the people scrolling past.

On BeReel, your work cannot be out-produced by slop — because slop cannot be posted at all. Every creator here is a person. Every post is a real moment. The feed does not reward volume. It rewards being there.

If you've been trying to make something real, and the internet has been burying it — this is where you post.
